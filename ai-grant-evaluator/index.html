Problem & Market Opportunity 20% weight 5.0
Crystal-clear problem with verified TAM ($1.5T global disruption cost), quantified customer pain (SGD 280K per stoppage), and strong market evidence from a 40-manufacturer survey. The mid-market gap between manual processes and enterprise tools is well-articulated and substantiated.
"In a survey we conducted across 40 Southeast Asian manufacturers in Q3 2024, 73% reported at least 3 unplanned production stoppages per year, each costing an average of SGD 280,000."
Solution & Innovation 20% weight 4.0
Multimodal sensor fusion with a proprietary 18-month SEA dataset and provisional patent provides meaningful differentiation. Two-week onboarding versus six to nine month enterprise deployments and a 10x price advantage are strong moat signals. Defensibility still partially relies on the patent prosecution outcome.
"We hold one provisional patent (filed March 2024) on our multimodal sensor fusion algorithm. A full patent application is in progress."
Business Model 15% weight 5.0
Exceptional unit economics: LTV/CAC of 7.5x, about a five-month payback period, and 72% gross margin at scale. The three-tier SaaS pricing model is clearly defined. A path to profitability in Q3 of Year 3 is credible given the current trajectory.
"LTV/CAC ratio: 7.5x | Gross margin: 72% | Payback period: ~5 months"
Traction & Validation 20% weight 4.0
Two paying customers under 12-month contracts with SGD 6K MRR and one Year 2 renewal with a quantified 34% downtime reduction. Two signed LOIs are in the pipeline. A 71% alert action rate suggests real product engagement. The jump to 10 customers in six months remains ambitious.
"Precision Parts Asia - SGD 3,200/month... Outcome: 34% reduction in unplanned stoppages; renewed for Year 2"
Team 15% weight 4.0
Strong founder-market fit: the CEO has eight years in supply chain operations plus a prior exit; the CTO brings ML expertise from Sea Group and two patents; the Head of Sales contributes a decade of ERP sales experience in SEA. The finance gap is acknowledged with a concrete hire plan. An academic advisor adds further credibility.
"Former ML lead at Sea Group; built real-time anomaly detection systems processing 800M events/day"
Financial Projections & Use of Funds 10% weight 4.0
Conservative three-year projections with explicit assumptions for ACV, churn, headcount, and infrastructure scaling. Fund allocation is specific across four categories and linked to milestones. The 20% churn assumption is consistent with pilot data, though still elevated for the segment.
"Milestones tied to grant: Month 2: 3 new pilot contracts signed... Month 6: 10 total paying customers; SGD 35K MRR achieved"

Strengths

What supports a pass
  • Quantified customer outcome of 34% downtime reduction from a paying Tier-1 pilot with Year 2 renewal.
  • Strong unit economics with LTV/CAC of 7.5x and roughly a five-month payback period.
  • Clear positioning gap in mid-market SEA versus US enterprise competitors, with a 10x price advantage and two-week onboarding.
  • Experienced founding team with domain expertise across supply chain, ML, and regional B2B sales, plus a prior exit.
  • Thoughtful risk acknowledgment, including PDPA and cross-border data handling, with visible mitigation planning.

Red Flags

What still needs scrutiny
  • 20% annual churn is elevated for B2B SaaS and could weaken the LTV assumptions if not brought down.
  • No CFO or finance lead yet, with billing currently handled by the Head of Sales.
  • The patent is only provisional, so part of the defensibility story still depends on prosecution success.
  • The grant milestone of 10 paying customers in six months is aggressive relative to the current base of two.

Due-Diligence Questions

What a reviewer should probe next
  • What is driving the current 20% annual churn and what interventions are expected to reduce it?
  • What qualified pipeline exists beyond the two LOIs to support the Month 6 target of 10 paying customers?
  • How dependent is the current product and data science workflow on the CTO's tacit knowledge?
  • What are the specific claims in the provisional patent and how defensible are they against prior art?
  • What is the post-grant financing plan to bridge the projected cumulative losses through Years 1 and 2?
